Navigation chrome

The child strip and swipe carousel use stable spatial order — deliberately different from the inbox's urgency order. The carousel below is live: drag it with your mouse or finger.

00

Child strip — mini-tabs, not icon dots

Name + status per child, nothing to memorize. Icon-only dots fail "recognition over recall" — identical glyphs are indistinguishable without memorizing position.

01

Swipe carousel — try it

Drag anywhere in the panel below. It tracks your finger 1:1, resists slightly past the edges, and settles with a spring — never a linear snap. Order is fixed (claude · cmd · tty) regardless of which one is most urgent.

Strip survives the keyboard; passive events don't move you

Two more constraints the chrome has to hold: the strip stays reachable with the keyboard open, and a passive (command) event may re-badge the strip without ever pulling the user away from what they're doing.
